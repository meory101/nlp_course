## 🎯 THE ONE-LINE POINT
> Self-correction fails NOT because models can't FIX errors but because they can't
> FIND them: the best model locates the mistake step only **~53%** of the time, yet
> given the TRUE location, fixing works (**+18 to +44 points**) and barely harms
> correct answers.

---

## ⭐ MY GRAB
> **Finding ≪ Fixing. Detection is the published bottleneck of self-correction —
> so an early, accurate detector (#18) is the missing input to a repair (#4) that
> already works. Bonus: a detector only ~60–70% accurate already makes repair
> pay off — the first data point on the pointer-accuracy price curve.**

---

## 🔧 THE MECHANIC ANALOGY
Car breaks down. Two skills: DIAGNOSIS (which part is broken?) vs REPAIR (replace
it). Everyone assumed LLMs are bad mechanics. Split the skills: they're **decent
mechanics, terrible diagnosticians**. Point at the broken part → repair goes fine.
Let them find it alone → they replace the radio while the engine is dead.

## 📝 WHAT IS A "TRACE"? (vocabulary)
A trace = the whole written "show your work": every step from problem to answer.
Example: `Problem (3+5)×2−4 → Step1: 3+5=8 ✅ → Step2: 8×2=18 ❌ (first error, should
be 16) → Step3: 18−4=14 → Answer 14 (wrong)`. One trace = one solved problem with
all its steps. (= my agent's TRANSCRIPT. trace/trajectory = the run; step = one line.)

## 🧱 THE DATASET — BIG-Bench Mistake (REUSABLE FOR ME)
- **2,186 CoT traces** (~5–25 steps each), generated by PaLM 2, on 5 deliberately
  clean tasks: word sorting, tracking shuffled objects, logical deduction,
  multi-step arithmetic, Dyck brackets. Chosen so mistakes are UNAMBIGUOUS.
- **Every trace labeled with the exact FIRST-error step** — human annotators
  (agreement α > 0.979 = near-perfect) on 4 tasks, algorithmic on brackets.
- ⚠️ Shape check: **multi-step trajectories with a gold failure-step label** —
  the first dataset besides Paper A's with real STEPS (E = single questions,
  I = one-shot math). Frozen text like Nautilus's traces, but labels are GOLD,
  not LLM-judged.

## 🔬 EXPERIMENT 1 — FINDING the mistake (the failure)
| | |
|---|---|
| **Mechanism** | Show the trace, ask "which step has the first error?" 3 prompt styles: whole-trace at once / per-step yes-no / per-step with reasoning. 3-shot, 5 models. |
| **Measure** | Accuracy at naming the labeled error step. |
| **Result** | GPT-4 **52.87%** (best) · GPT-4-Turbo 48.3 · PaLM 2 Unicorn 24.7 · Gemini Pro 16.1 · GPT-3.5 14.8. |
| **Conclusion** | Even GPT-4 misses HALF the errors — on artificial tasks with unambiguous mistakes. Same lesson as Paper I's 10/57/82% spread: finding is unreliable AND model-idiosyncratic. |

## 🔬 EXPERIMENT 2 — FIXING with the true location (the success)
| | |
|---|---|
| **Mechanism** | **BACKTRACKING** (= my old idea #21, checkpoint-and-rollback): keep the trace up to the oracle-given error step → regenerate that step at temperature=1 until it differs (max 8 tries) → continue the rest at temperature=0. |
| **Measure** | Δaccuracy separately on originally-WRONG vs originally-CORRECT traces (the over-correction guard). |
| **Result** | Wrong traces: **+18 to +44 points** (tracking +43.9, logic +36.9). Correct traces: tiny damage (0 to −11) — "minimal effect". **Control:** backtrack from a RANDOM step → gains collapse (+20 vs +44) → it's the LOCATION doing the work, not retrying. |
| **Conclusion** | Repair is robust once the pointer is accurate. |

## 🔬 EXPERIMENT 3 — small trained external detector (quiet but key for #18)
- Fine-tuned a SMALLER model to do ONE job — spot the error step. Trained on 4 of
  the 5 tasks, tested on the held-out 5th (proves it learned "what mistakes look
  like", not memorized a task): **beat every prompted big model** on the unseen
  task (+22.6 on brackets).
- Simulation: a detector at only **~60–70% accuracy already makes backtracking
  profitable** — no perfect alarm needed.
- → External, dedicated detector > the model introspecting = published support for
  Paper I's GAP 13 (external model-agnostic detector = my #18).
- ⚠️ NOT released (Google-private base model) + post-mortem + needs training →
  I don't use it; I cite it.

## 🗺️ THE WHOLE MACHINE IN ONE TABLE (don't mis-read: only 2 are real detectors)
The fixer (backtracking) is ONE machine; they fed it pointers from 4 SOURCES:

| Pointer source | Real detector? | Job in the experiment | Fixing result |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1. Human answer key | ❌ (ground truth) | **CEILING** — "if pointing were perfect, does fixing work?" | **+18..+44**, no harm |
| 2. Random step | ❌ (sanity check) | **FLOOR** — "is it location, or just retrying?" | gains collapse (+20 vs +44) |
| 3. Model itself | ✅ detector #1 | realistic self-correction | bad eyes, 53% best |
| 4. Small trained model | ✅ detector #2 | their proposal | ~60–70%, beats big models |

Flashlight test in a dark room: row 1 = turn on the room lights, row 2 = no light
at all, rows 3–4 = the two actual flashlights compared between those ends.
**The fixer is constant; only the eyes change → detection quality IS repair quality.**

## ⚖️ THE I-vs-K TENSION — RESOLVED (both sides now read)
- **K** fed **TRUE** locations (human gold) → fixing works (+44), barely harms.
- **I** fed the model's **OWN GUESSED** (mostly wrong) locations → hurt 2/3 models.
- **Combined law: accurate pointer helps a lot; wrong pointer is worse than
  silence.** → Repair quality is a FUNCTION of detection quality → **#18 prices #4.**
  Strongest single justification of the Detect→Repair arc; K's 60–70% threshold is
  the first point on that price curve.

---

## 🕳️ MY ONE-LINE GAP (I said it myself, keep it in my words)
> **"They detect and correct AFTER the complete failure."**
Everything is post-mortem: run finishes → autopsy → repair-retry. Nobody detects
DURING the run, fires BEFORE failure completes, or measures whether EARLIER = better
repair. Same hole shape as D ("detects in real-time, doesn't forecast") and E
("single questions, no steps"). Three papers, one empty seat = my lead-time idea.

## ⚠️ HONEST LIMITS
1. **Artificial tasks** — their own words: "artificial and unrealistic for
   real-world applications." Word sorting ≠ a real agent run.
2. **CoT reasoning text, not agents** — no tools, no observations, no environment,
   no self-conditioning pressure.
3. **Post-mortem oracle** — location handed AFTER the trace is finished.
4. **First error only, one per trace**; 85% of traces are failures (skewed).
5. **Old setup** (PaLM 2 traces, 2023/24 finders) — the NUMBERS are era-specific;
   the durable claim is the SPLIT (find ≪ fix).
6. No cost accounting (backtracking ≤8 retries never compared vs full-rerun at
   equal budget).
